# premortem: route --as blocked

## premortem: imagine we shipped and it failed

project forward: we shipped this, and it failed badly. why?

1. **robots abuse blocked**: robots mark blocked too frequently, use it as an easy exit, humans get overwhelmed with low-quality escalations
2. **articulation is shallow**: robots write "i'm blocked" without real reflection, humans get no useful context
3. **humans miss blocked stones**: route.drive output is ignored, blocked stones pile up, robots stay stuck

---

## what obvious signs did we ignore?

- robots are good at pattern-match; they might game the two-step flow without genuine reflection
- humans already ignore a lot of terminal output; blocked might blend in
- articulation quality depends on robot capability; weak robots write weak articulation

---

## what assumptions could turn out wrong?

| assumption | what if wrong? | likelihood |
|------------|----------------|------------|
| robots will read the nudge and reflect | they might skip to articulation mechanically | medium |
| humans will check route.drive regularly | blocked stones could go unnoticed for days | medium |
| articulation forces genuine reflection | robots might write boilerplate without insight | low |
| two-step flow provides enough friction | robots might automate around it | low |
| blocked is a temporary state | robots might never unblock, which creates zombie stones | low |

---

## what is the nightmare scenario?

- **worst plausible outcome**: robots learn that `--as blocked` is an escape hatch. they mark blocked immediately when stuck, without any attempt to solve the problem. humans return to find 50 blocked stones with shallow articulations. trust in robot autonomy erodes. humans start to micro-manage, which defeats the purpose of route automation.

- **what would cause the most damage**: loss of trust in robot escalation. if blocked is abused, humans will ignore it or disable the feature.

- **what would be hardest to recover from**: cultural shift. once humans learn to distrust blocked status, to re-establish trust is slow.

---

## what mitigations to consider?

| risk | mitigation | cost of mitigation |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| robots abuse blocked | nudge with "failure is only the opportunity to begin again" + required articulation | low (implemented) |
| shallow articulation | structured articulation format (what blocks, what tried, what needs) | low (implemented) |
| humans miss blocked | route.drive prominently shows blocked status and path | low (implemented) |
| rapid abuse | could add time delay like selfreview (not implemented, may add friction) | medium |
| zombie stones | could add timeout or stale detection (not implemented, future work) | medium |

---

## assessment

risks are mitigated by:
1. two-step flow (trigger → articulate → confirm)
2. nudge content that encourages reflection
3. structured articulation format
4. route.drive visibility

residual risks are acceptable for v1. monitor for abuse patterns is appropriate follow-up work.
