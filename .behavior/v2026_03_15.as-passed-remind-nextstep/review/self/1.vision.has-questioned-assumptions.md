# self-review: 1.vision.has-questioned-assumptions

## assumptions questioned

### assumption 1: drivers always want to continue after pass

**what do we assume here?**
that after a stone is passed, the driver wants to proceed to the next stone

**what evidence supports this?**
- routes are linear progressions
- stones are checkpoints toward a goal
- the wish specifically describes the "now what?" confusion

**what if the opposite were true?**
if drivers wanted to stop after each stone, the reminder would be unwelcome. but even then, it's just information — they can ignore it.

**did the wisher actually say this?**
yes — "so they dont get confused and try to go down a wrong direction" implies they want to continue

**exceptions?**
- driver may want to take a break — reminder is still harmless
- driver may want to review prior stones — reminder doesn't prevent this

**verdict:** assumption holds ✓

---

### assumption 2: `rhx route.drive` is the canonical next command

**what do we assume here?**
that route.drive is the correct command to continue

**what evidence supports this?**
- wisher explicitly says "run `rhx route.drive` to continue the drive"
- route.drive is the established driver loop command

**what if the opposite were true?**
if a different command were canonical, the reminder would be wrong. but the wisher specified this exact command.

**did the wisher actually say this?**
yes, explicitly

**verdict:** assumption holds ✓

---

### assumption 3: a single line reminder is sufficient

**what do we assume here?**
that one line of text ("run rhx route.drive") is enough guidance

**what evidence supports this?**
- the wish asks for exactly this — minimal, direct guidance
- drivers who need more help can run `rhx help` or read docs

**what if the opposite were true?**
if drivers needed more context (e.g., what route.drive does), the reminder would be insufficient. but:
- to add explanation would bloat output
- drivers who reached `--as passed` already know the flow
- the wish asks specifically for this minimal reminder

**exceptions?**
- first-time users might need more — but they likely won't reach passage without prior guidance
- the reminder is a nudge, not a tutorial

**verdict:** assumption holds ✓

---

### assumption 4: drivers get confused after pass (the problem exists)

**what do we assume here?**
that drivers actually experience the "now what?" confusion

**what evidence supports this?**
- wisher observed this and filed the wish
- i personally just experienced it — tried to pass a stone and wondered what to do next

**what if the opposite were true?**
if drivers always knew to run route.drive, this feature would be unnecessary noise. but the wish exists because confusion was observed.

**verdict:** assumption holds (validated by wisher observation and my own experience) ✓

---

### assumption 5: the reminder won't bother experienced users

**what do we assume here?**
that extra output is acceptable even to users who don't need it

**what evidence supports this?**
- one line of text is minimal noise
- experienced users can scan past it
- consistency is valuable — same output every time

**what if the opposite were true?**
if users found it bothersome, they might request a way to disable it. the vision notes this as acceptable risk: "experienced users may find it redundant (but harmless)"

**verdict:** assumption acceptable — risk acknowledged ✓
