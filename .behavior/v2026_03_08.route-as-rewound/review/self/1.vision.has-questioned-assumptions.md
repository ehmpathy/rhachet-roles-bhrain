# self-review: has-questioned-assumptions

## assumption 1: "rewound" is the right term

**what did wisher say?** "rewind back to the entrypoint"

**what did i infer?** past tense "rewound" fits the `--as <past-participle>` pattern (passed, approved, promised)

**what if the opposite were true?** maybe "rewound" is awkward because it implies time travel, but we're not restoring old state — we're clear state to enable fresh forward progress.

**alternatives not considered**:
- `--as reset` — aligns with "reset to start"
- `--as voided` — clearer that validation is void
- `--as zeroed` — "back to zero"

**verdict**: assumption may be wrong. should validate term with wisher. "rewound" is my choice, not theirs.

## assumption 2: rewound is human-only

**what did wisher say?** "after the human does their part, they want to rewind"

**what did i infer?** therefore agents can't rewind — like approval

**what if the opposite were true?** there could be valid automation scenarios:
- ci/cd pipeline retries on transient failures
- agent detects "external dependency now available" and rewinds
- scheduled re-evaluation of old stones

**evidence against human-only**: the wish describes a human scenario but doesn't say ONLY humans. the analogy to approval may be flawed — approval grants permission, rewind clears state. different semantics.

**verdict**: assumption is weak. should ask wisher explicitly.

## assumption 3: all validation types must be cleared together

**what did wisher say?** "invalidate reviews and triggers and etc"

**what did i infer?** "etc" means all validation types — reviews, judges, promises, triggers, approval

**what if the opposite were true?** maybe wisher only meant the specific blockers for their scenario. maybe approval should survive since a human granted it.

**counterexample**: human approves stone, agent runs review that fails, agent blocks. human fixes, wants to rewind. should the human re-approve? or should their prior approval count?

**verdict**: assumption needs more nuance. "clear all" is safe but maybe not optimal.

## assumption 4: passage.jsonl should record "rewound" status

**what did wisher say?** no mention of audit trail

**what did i infer?** consistency with how other states (passed, approved) are logged

**what if we didn't log it?**

- but also... do we actually need to know a stone was rewound? once it passes again, the passage record shows "passed" and that's what matters

**alternative**: don't log rewound at all — just delete the validation artifacts and let the next passage attempt create fresh records

**verdict**: assumption adds complexity. may not be needed. should ask wisher if audit trail matters.

## assumption 5: one stone at a time (no cascade)

**what did wisher say?** "rewind back to the entrypoint to a route" and "start the stone over"

**what did i infer?** rewound affects a single stone

**what if the opposite were true?**
- if you rewind stone 3, stones 4+ may have been validated on assumptions that stone 3 was valid
- maybe rewind should cascade forward: rewind 3, auto-rewind 4, 5, etc
- or maybe wisher meant "rewind the whole route from stone X forward"

**the wish says**: "rewind back to the entrypoint to a route" — this sounds like route-level, not stone-level!

**critical insight**: i may have misread the scope. the wish might mean "rewind the ROUTE to the start of a stone" not "rewind a single stone". these are different!

**wisher confirmed**: CASCADE FROM STONE. rewind the specified stone AND all subsequent stones.

**verdict**: assumption was WRONG. my original assumption (single stone, no cascade) was corrected by wisher. the cascade behavior makes sense: downstream stones were validated while upstream was valid.
