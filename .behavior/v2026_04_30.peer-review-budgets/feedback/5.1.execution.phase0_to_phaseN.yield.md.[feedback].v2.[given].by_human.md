# blocker.1


1. High: cached approved reviews can turn into exhaustion blocks once `rounds >= budget`.
In [runStoneGuardReviews.ts](/home/vlad/git/ehmpathy/_worktrees/rhachet-roles-bhrain.vlad.peer-review-budgets/src/domain.operations/route/guard/runStoneGuardReviews.ts:247), exhaustion is computed with `blockers: Infinity` before the cached approved review check at line 278. That diverges from the wish: “if that review has said all good already, then its already cached and budget wont be used.” Repro shape: reviewer budget `1`, first review passes and consumes round `1/1`, later same-hash pass sees `verdict === exhausted` before cache approval and can trigger the implicit approval gate.

2. High: structured peer reviews without `budget` are silently discarded instead of defaulting to unlimited.
The vision says missing `budget` should be backward compatible/unlimited. But [parseStoneGuard.ts](/home/vlad/git/ehmpathy/_worktrees/rhachet-roles-bhrain.vlad.peer-review-budgets/src/domain.operations/route/guard/parseStoneGuard.ts:72) only finalizes a structured peer if `budget !== undefined`. A guard entry with `slug` and `run` but no budget disappears entirely, meaning the review never runs. This is a sharp omission and needs an acceptance test.

3. Medium: `route.guard.budget --add N` defaults to editing every guard in the route, not the current stone.
The help/comment says `--stone` defaults to current stone, but [route.ts](/home/vlad/git/ehmpathy/_worktrees/rhachet-roles-bhrain.vlad.peer-review-budgets/src/contract/cli/route.ts:1768) sets `targetGuards` to all guard files when `stoneName` is absent. That is ergonomic friction with real blast radius: a human trying to unstick one exhausted reviewer may accidentally raise budgets across unrelated future stones.

4. Medium: direct `rhx route.drive` does not show the peer budget state or exhaustion prompt.
The envisioned contract says users monitor rounds via `rhx route.drive`, and acceptance text expects direct `route.drive` to show exhausted/approved reviewers. But direct mode at [stepRouteDrive.ts](/home/vlad/git/ehmpathy/_worktrees/rhachet-roles-bhrain.vlad.peer-review-budgets/src/domain.operations/route/stepRouteDrive.ts:295) always calls the generic formatter, which only prints stone guidance. Budget-exhausted messaging is hook-only at lines 160 and 226. The multilevel acceptance test includes `route.drive` expectations, but there is no committed multilevel snapshot, so this friction is not clearly covered.
