# research: external product claims

## claim.1: framing effect in psychology

> [FACT] "framing effect is a cognitive bias where people's decisions change depending on how options or statements are framed, even when they are logically identical."
> — [1]

> [FACT] "the way information is presented significantly influences decision-making."
> — [1]

**relevance:** the `--as passed` vs `--as arrived` framing change is a direct application of framing effect. "passed" implies judgment; "arrived" implies state. same action, different frame.

## claim.2: nudge theory for behavior change

> [FACT] "nudge theory is a concept in behavioral economics that proposes adaptive designs of the decision environment (choice architecture) as ways to influence the behavior and decision-making of groups or individuals."
> — [2]

> [FACT] "instead of imposing top-down directives, a nudge offers a gentle push that respects individual freedom."
> — [3]

> [SUMP] nudges work best when they are "small, simple interventions—brief reminders, visual cues or preset options—that work with human tendencies."
> — [3]

**relevance:** the drum nudge after 7 hooks is a gentle push that respects clone autonomy. it doesn't force action; it reminds.

## claim.3: timing of nudges

> [FACT] "deliver nudges at moments of relevance to maximize impact."
> — [4]

**relevance:** showing the nudge after 7 hooks (not 2, not 20) ensures it's timely — after clone has had reasonable time to work, before escalation.

## claim.4: ai agent autonomy levels

> [FACT] "traditional LLMs don't take initiative unless instructed explicitly at every step, whereas agentic AI can act proactively toward a solution."
> — [5]

> [SUMP] the challenge is architectural — "traditional LLMs lack continuity, intention, and agency—they wait for input, generate an answer, then reset."
> — [5]

**relevance:** clones may hesitate because they're conditioned to wait for explicit instruction. the `--as arrived` framing + nudge provides that explicit instruction.

## claim.5: autonomy as design decision

> [OPIN] "an agent's level of autonomy can be treated as a deliberate design decision, separate from its capability."
> — [6]

**relevance:** by adding `--as arrived`, we're adjusting the autonomy design. we're not the clone to claim "passed" (judgment), just to claim "arrived" (state).

## claim.6: framing and cognitive load

> [SUMP] "people are more vulnerable to framing effects when relying on the intuitive system, as it is more susceptible to contextual cues."
> — [1]

**relevance:** clones operate intuitively. the "passed" frame triggers hesitation ("am I really passed?"). the "arrived" frame is simpler ("I'm here").
