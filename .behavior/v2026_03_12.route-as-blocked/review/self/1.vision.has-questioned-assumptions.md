## deeper reflection

### what did questioning assumptions reveal?

1. **the core contract is solid** — `--as blocked` + nudge are directly from wish
2. **recovery path is undefined** — wish doesn't specify how to unblock
3. **notification gap exists** — no mechanism to alert humans of blocked state
4. **interactive assumption is the biggest risk** — many robots run non-interactively

### what would I do differently next time?

- explicitly ask: "how do we recover from blocked state?"
- clarify: "who is the foreman?"
- propose: `--force` escape hatch earlier, not as afterthought

### what surprised me?

the wish is focused on **signal** (robot can say they're blocked) but silent on **response** (what happens next). the vision fills this gap with assumptions. these should be validated.
