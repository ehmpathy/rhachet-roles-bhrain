# 1.vision — the learner as a continuous compiler of this repo's domain terms

> the learner already has a **core trait**: the incessant thirst to **crystallize and
> externalize lessons for durable retention** — so a lesson hardwon once is never lost, and every
> future traveler inherits it. it already externalizes **lessons** (→ briefs) and **tactics** (→
> skills).
>
> a **domain.term is a specific type of lesson** — the lesson of *what this domain calls a
> concept*. so **this wish is not a new trait — it is that same trait applied to one special kind
> of lesson**: the domain-term lesson. a general lesson lands as a brief; a **domain-term lesson
> lands in `.agent/repo=.this/role=any/domain.terms/`** — its own home because these lessons are
> structured (one canonical term, its forbidden synonyms) and worth their own dictionary.
>
> **plus a hook that proactively nudges the trait**: an onStop sweephook that reminds the learner
> to distill its domain-term lessons when it has been a while. so — whenever a domain term is
> **discovered or debated** — the learner's nature is to capture that lesson, org-wide, for all
> who come after.
>
> the hook is that obsession made mechanical, and it mirrors a very human habit: **every so
> often — say, once an hour — a person pauses to reflect on what they have been through, notes
> the lessons worth keeping for consistency, and in doing so raises the floor for everyone around
> them.** the onStop sweephook is that same habit, made explicit: after a good round of work,
> once the distillation has gone **stale (> 1 hour, walltime)**, it reminds the learner to take a
> moment, reflect, and distill. where a human's itch is **subconscious**, here it just happens to
> be **explicit** — surfaced plainly as **stophook instructions** (the nudge the hook prints) and
> articulated openly in `progress.md`. it holds the *stop* open (via exit code) so the reminder
> is not missed; it **never blocks writes**. 🍵
>
> the dictionary it builds is scoped to exactly the terms we care about: the repo's own **domain
> objects** and **domain operations** (the `DomainEntity`/`DomainLiteral` nouns and the
> `domain.operation` verbs *declared in this repo*). those — and only those — are what "born
> here" means. all else (the synonym-forbid, the dir scaffold, the hook) serves that one
> itemization.

## the outcome world

### day-in-the-life

a clone works a route in `rhachet-roles-bhrain`. mid-flow it **declares a new domain object**
`DomainTerm` (a `DomainLiteral`) and a **new domain operation** `genDomainTerm`, and casually
calls something a "checkpoint" in a comment — even though the repo already calls that concept
a `stone`.

the session winds down. the clone signals it's done. the **onStop sweephook** offers a gentle
reminder:

- it reads `.agent/.cache/repo=bhrain/role=learner/skill=learn.domain.terms/progress.md`
- the file was last mutated **> 1 hour ago** (stale) → the hook holds the stop open (exit 2) —
  a nudge to pause, never a block on any write
- the owl nudge lands on stderr: *"🦉 good round. take a moment — distill your domain.term
  lessons before you rest. 🍵"*

the nudge does no work on its own — **the learner decides** what the round was worth. it runs
`rhx learn.domain.terms` and reflects on the terms its new dobjs/dops introduced this session —
it declared the object `DomainTerm` and the operation `genDomainTerm`, so it splits those into
constituent words:

- `gen`, `route`, `guard`, `stone` — terms already in the dictionary → no-op (findsert finds them)
- the noun `domain-term` — **new word, born of the `DomainTerm` object + `genDomainTerm`
  operation** → the learner writes the say-level `domain.terms/term=domain-term._.choice._.md`,
  its ref-level `term=domain-term._.choice.reason.md`, and lists both declarations in the
  choice's `.refs`. (`gen` is reused, not re-created — its refs just gain `genDomainTerm`.)
- `"checkpoint"` used for `stone` → the learner judges it a synonym, records it in
  `term=stone._.choice._.md` (forbidden in contracts; a comment may keep it for an alternate angle)
  and, since it was arguable, logs a resolved **dispute** in `term=stone._.choice.reason.md`. had
  `checkpoint` named a dobj/dop or a published interface, *that* would be the contract-violation
  to fix — left in place until disturbed

it articulates the outcome into `progress.md` — *what it distilled, and why* (and if it judged
there were no new terms, **why none**). that re-write refreshes the mtime, so the next stop
rests. the **only hard requirement** is this articulation; every judgment above is the learner's
to make. 🍵

### before / after

| | before | after |
|---|--------|-------|
| where terms live | in people's heads + scattered code | one dir: `domain.terms/` |
| synonym drift | silent, accretes forever | the learner catches + records it when it distills |
| new domain nouns/verbs | named ad-hoc, never cataloged | the learner itemizes them, nudged hourly |
| dictionary upkeep | a human remembers, rarely | onStop sweephook — a gentle reminder to distill (holds the stop, never writes) |
| glossary freshness | rots the moment it's written | self-heals every ~hour of work |

### the "aha"

the value clicks when a second clone, months later, boots into the repo and **already
speaks the language** — `domain.terms/` booted into its context. it never invents "client"
for `customer`, never says "task" for `job`, because the terms — and their forbidden
synonyms — are right there. the glossary stopped being a doc someone forgets to update
and became a **living compilation artifact**, the same way `briefs/` and `skills/` are.

## user experience

### usecases & goals

the primary "user" is a **clone (or human) who works in this repo**. goals:

1. **itemize the domain's vocabulary — the whole point** — every **domain term** (a **noun**,
   **verb**, or **adj**) that composes the repo's **declared domain objects & operations** gets a
   file. one entry per word, **reused** across every object/operation that uses it — the noun
   `stone` serves `Stone`, `getStone`, `genStone` alike; the verb `gen` serves every `gen*`. the
   dobjs/dops are the *anchor* (they are what "born here" means); the **term is the word** they
   are built from.
2. **keep contracts on the canonical term** — dobj/dop names, internal contracts, and above all
   published external interfaces must obey the `domain.terms/` canon; a synonym in a *contract* is
   the violation (a synonym in a comment, for an alternate-perspective explanation, is fine).
3. **let drift self-heal** — never hand-audit the glossary; the learner distills it.
4. **onboard fast** — a fresh traveler inherits this domain's vocabulary on boot.

### contract inputs & outputs

**A. the zoom-in identity brief** `im_an.obsessive_learner.for.domain.terms.md` — sits **right
next to the extant `im_an.obsessive_learner.md`** in `src/domain.roles/learner/briefs/`, booted
every session via `boot.yml`. it is a **zoom-in** of the learner's identity onto one special
kind of lesson:

- it makes **crystal clear** that this is a **core trait**: *the domain-term lesson is a
  specific type of lesson the learner is obsessed with capturing.* whenever a domain term is
  **discovered or debated** — a new domain object/operation named, a synonym argued, a canonical
  word settled — the learner's nature is to **crystallize that lesson and externalize it** into
  `domain.terms/`, for **durable retention** by **all future travelers, org-wide**.
- it specializes the extant identity, not replaces it: the general `im_an.obsessive_learner.md`
  says "crystallize lessons"; this zoom-in says "and domain-term lessons are one you obsess over,
  and here is where they go." a term discovered or debated but not externalized is a lesson lost.

**B. the two rules** — authored **in the learner role briefs** (`src/domain.roles/learner/briefs/`),
the single source of truth; booted every session:

- `rule.require.domain-term-itemization` (blocker) — **the foundational scope.** every **domain
  term** — a **noun/verb/adj** that composes the repo's declared domain objects & operations —
  must be itemized into `domain.terms/`. one entry per word, reused across every dobj/dop that
  uses it. the declared dobjs/dops are the anchor; the itemized unit is the term (the word).
- `rule.forbid.domain-term-synonyms` (blocker) — the **contract** must use the canonical term:
  dobj/dop names, internal contracts, and **especially the external interfaces we publish**. a
  synonym in a *contract* is the violation — adhere, or open a dispute. a synonym in a
  **comment**, to describe the concept from an alternate perspective, is allowed; the term file
  records it so the canonical map stays clear.

**where the rules live — symlink, not copy.** the rules stay in the learner role briefs (one
source of truth, versioned with the role). into `domain.terms/` we findsert only a **symlink**
back to each rule brief — so the dir advertises its own governance without a divergent copy.
this matches the repo's extant symlink convention (e.g.
`.agent/repo=.this/role=any/briefs/im_a.bhrain_owl.md` is a git-tracked symlink, mode `120000`,
into `src/domain.roles/driver/briefs/`).

the zoom-in identity brief (A) declares the trait; the two rules (B) are its enforceable form. all are
**mandates**, not a write-time gate — honored by the learner as it distills (nudged by the
onStop sweephook), by anyone who cleans up a contract-synonym when they next touch its path, and
at review — **never by a hook that blocks a file write.**

**C. the glossary dir** `.agent/repo=.this/role=any/domain.terms/` — bootstrapped by a
**deterministic findsert** (per the wish) that lays down:

- `.readme.md` — explains the dir + the rules; findserted so it always exists
- **symlinks** to the two rule briefs in the learner role (not copies) — so the mandate is
  visible in the dir it governs while its source of truth stays in `src/domain.roles/learner/`
- then, per term, **two files at two levels** — the **choice** (say) and its **reason** (ref):

**the choice — `term=stone._.choice._.md` (say-level: concise, always booted into context)** —
proposed template for your review:

```md
# domain.term: stone

term.chosen   = stone
term.kind     = noun                 # noun | verb | adj  (the part of speech, reused across objects & operations)
term.synonyms.forbidden:
- checkpoint
- milestone-marker
- waypoint

## .what
a single milestone on a route.

## .refs
where the term is declared / used, plus notable examples (a term is reused across many):
- src/domain.objects/Stone.ts            # the domain object
- src/domain.operations/route/getStone.ts
- src/domain.operations/route/genStone.ts

## .reason
see the ref-level cluster beside this choice:
- `term=stone._.choice.reason.md` — etymology, disputes, evidence
- `term=stone._.choice.example=<abc>.md` — one file per usage example (e.g.
  `term=stone._.choice.example=trail-marker.md`, `term=stone._.choice.example=vs-guard.md`)
```

> **why the `term=<x>._.choice.*` shape** — `term=<x>._` marks the term itself as a **cluster
> root** (so future facets beyond `choice` can be added later, e.g. `term=<x>._.usage.*`).
> within it, `.choice.*` is the term-choice facet: a lean say-level `.choice._.md` root, plus
> ref-level members `.choice.reason.md` and one-per-example `.choice.example=<abc>.md`.

**the reason — `term=stone._.choice.reason.md` (ref-level: deep, surfaced on demand)** —
**required** so the say-level choice stays lean while the full record of *why* lives on. proposed
template:

```md
# domain.term.choice.reason: stone

## .etymology
why `stone`: the route domain is a *path* of markers; a stone is a marker you pass on a trail.
chosen over `checkpoint` (implies a gate/validation — that is the `guard`'s job) and `waypoint`
(navigation jargon, not the trail metaphor).

## .disputes
### dispute: checkpoint  —  raised 2026-07-22  —  status: RESOLVED (keep `stone`)
- raised.by  = <traveler>
- claim      = "checkpoint" reads clearer to newcomers than "stone"
- counter    = "checkpoint" implies a *gate that validates*; that concept already exists as
               `guard`. a `stone` merely *marks progress*. to merge them would overload one word
               onto two distinct concepts (rule.forbid.term.addition.ambiguous).
- resolution = keep `stone`; record `checkpoint` as a forbidden synonym. dispute closed.

## .evidence
- discovery: <dimensional decomposition / scenario narrative / citation — per architect howto>
- invariants: <forbidden combinations / states / refs, if a dobj>
```

> the **dispute** pattern above is the proposed convention: a dispute is a dated, resolvable
> entry **in the `term=<x>._.choice.reason.md` file** — raised / claim / counter / resolution. a separate
> learner **`howto.domain-term-disputes` brief** + a **`template.domain-term` brief** (the two
> blocks above) establish the pattern reusably. both are for your review.

**D. the sweep skill** `rhx learn.domain.terms` — a **guide for the learner's reflection**, not
an autonomous distiller. it **does no work on its own**; the **learner decides** what to capture.

- input (hints, not rules): the domain objects & operations declared/engaged this session — the
  learner may lean on the git diff of touched files, but *what counts as "engaged with"* is the
  learner's call
- output, as the learner decides: the say-level **choice files** (`term=<x>._.choice._.md`) + their
  required ref-level **reason files** (`term=<x>._.choice.reason.md`, and any
  `term=<x>._.choice.example=<abc>.md`) + a rewritten
  `.agent/.cache/repo=bhrain/role=learner/skill=learn.domain.terms/progress.md`
- the **one hard requirement** is `progress.md`: the learner's **articulation of their
  domain.term lesson progress** — what they distilled and **why** — and if they judged there were
  no new terms, **why none this round**. that re-write refreshes the mtime, so the next stop
  rests. every other judgment (which terms, which synonyms, which disputes) is the learner's.

**E. the onStop sweephook** (the wish's term) — registered in `getLearnerRole.ts` under
`hooks.onBrain.onStop`, invoked as **`rhx learn.domain.terms --when hook.onStop`**, in line with
the driver's extant `route.drive --when hook.onStop`:

- its **sole trigger** is the mtime of the **exact** cache file
  `.agent/.cache/repo=bhrain/role=learner/skill=learn.domain.terms/progress.md` — path
  conformance matters: the hook keys off this precise path and only this path
- if mutated **within the last hour** → exit 0 (let the session rest)
- if **stale (> 1 hour)** → exit 2 to **hold the stop open** (not any write) + an owl nudge to
  stderr that reminds the learner to take a moment and distill the domain.term lessons gathered

so the same skill has two faces (like the driver's `route.drive`): the **hook face**
(`--when hook.onStop`) is the 5s staleness check + gentle nudge; the **clone face**
(`rhx learn.domain.terms`) does the actual reflect-and-distill. gentle by design: after a good
round of work, a stale distillation reminds the learner to pause and tend the dictionary before
they move on. it holds the *stop* until they distill; it never touches a *write*.

### a leverage timeline

```
t0  clone works, declares/debates some domain terms
t1  clone signals stop
t2  onStop sweephook: progress.md stale (> 1hr)? → exit 2, hold stop, gentle nudge
t3  learner runs `rhx learn.domain.terms`, reflects, and DECIDES what to capture
t4  learner writes choice + reason files as it judges fit, and articulates progress.md
t5  clone signals stop again → progress.md fresh → exit 0 → rest 🌙
```

## mental model

### how a user describes it to a friend

> "the repo has an auto-updating dictionary of its **domain objects and operations**. every
> time i wrap up, it checks whether that dictionary is stale — if so it won't let me *leave*
> (it never blocks my edits) until i distill the objects and operations i declared into it. if
> i named a **contract** with a word the repo already has a canonical name for, it records mine
> as a forbidden synonym so nobody drifts."

### analogies / metaphors

- **the obsessive itch, made explicit** — the strongest one: a human feels a subconscious itch
  to set a thing right before they can rest. the learner has the same itch to distill — and here
  it is surfaced *explicitly*, as the stophook's instructions, instead of left subconscious.
- **a specialized lesson-brief** — a domain-term entry *is* a lesson (like any brief), just a
  structured subtype: one canonical term + its forbidden synonyms, kept in its own dictionary.
- **a linter for vocabulary** — synonyms are lint errors; the sweep is the lint pass
- **a garden's seed catalog** (architect's domain-as-a-garden) — each discovered term is
  a labeled seed, kept so the next planting reuses it

### their terms vs our terms

| they say | we say |
|----------|--------|
| "dictionary" / "glossary" | `domain.terms/` (this domain's glossary dir) |
| "banned word" | forbidden **synonym** |
| "the real name" | the **chosen** / canonical term |
| "a nudge when i wrap up" | the **onStop sweephook** |
| "catalog the word" | **findsert** the term (deterministic) |
| "tidy up the words i used" | **sweep** the terms |

## evaluation

### how well it solves the goals

- **compile this domain's language continuously** ✓ — the sweep is the compile step; the hook makes it
  recur without human prompting
- **ground in architect lessons** ✓ — the rules are the learner-scale echo of the
  architect's `rule.forbid.term.addition.synonym` + `rule.require.persist-domain-term-evidence`
  + `rule.require.domain-discovery-for-term-proposals`
- **deterministic findsert** ✓ — re-running the sweep on an established term is a no-op;
  idempotent by construction (matches `rule.require.idempotent-operations`)
- **a reminder with teeth, not just aspiration** ✓ — the gentle nudge lands because onStop
  exit-2 holds the stop; a proven mechanism (driver's `route.drive` uses it today)

### pros

- glossary can never rot more than ~1 hour past active work
- new travelers inherit the whole language on boot (files live under `role=any/`)
- synonyms become **data** (recorded, forbidden) instead of tribal knowledge
- reuses three proven patterns: learner hook-registration, `memory.guard.sh` skill
  wrapper, driver onStop exit-2 stop-hold

### cons / tradeoffs

- **a pause at stop** — even a gentle reminder interrupts the moment of "done"; the 1-hour
  grace window + the frame (a moment to distill, not a block) soften it, but the threshold is a
  guess (see open questions)
- **reflection quality** — "which dobjs/dops did i declare this session" is a fuzzy input; the
  git diff + `DomainEntity`/`domain.operation` signals help, but the synonym call is the hard part
- **synonym false-positives** — an over-eager synonym call could flag a legitimate *distinct*
  concept that merely looks alike; the dispute path is the escape valve

### edgecases & pit-of-success

| edgecase | how the contract keeps you safe |
|----------|--------------------------------|
| generic/common word (e.g. "file", "run") | dictionary scopes to **words that compose declared dobjs/dops** → a generic word not part of the domain vocabulary is not itemized |
| the same noun across many operations (`getStone`, `genStone`) | **one** term entry (`stone`), reused — the refs list enumerates all its declarations, no duplicate files |
| term already established | findsert is a no-op → idempotent, no churn |
| legitimate near-synonym that is a *distinct* concept | **dispute** path: adhere or open into dispute, not silent-block |
| extant code full of a forbidden synonym | flagged, **left in place until disturbed** — no forced mass-rewrite |
| cache dir missing on first run | absent/older-than-hour → treated as stale → sweep runs (fail toward sweeping) |
| clock skew / mtime unreliable | mtime is the signal per the wish; note as a validate-later risk |
| no new terms this round | the learner still articulates `progress.md` — and **justifies why none** — which refreshes its mtime → stop rests |

## open questions & assumptions

### assumptions made

1. **onStop hooks fire per-role and can force exit 2** — verified: driver registers
   `onStop` and `stepRouteDrive.ts:163` blocks with exit 2 on `hook.onStop`
2. **`.agent/.cache/repo=bhrain/role=learner/skill=learn.domain.terms/progress.md`** is the
   staleness sentinel (its mtime) — the wish's cache-path convention, with the skill renamed to
   `learn.domain.terms`. **exact path conformance is required**: the hook keys off this precise
   path, so the sweep must write to exactly it
3. **the sweep reflects on session context** to decide which terms were engaged — the wish
   says "reflect on the terms used or engaged with"
4. **term files live under `role=any/`** so they boot for every role, not just the learner
   — consistent with where `briefs/` + `skills/` durable capture already lands
5. **findsert semantics** = find-or-create, idempotent (get-set-gen-verbs `gen`)

### open questions — triaged (`[answered]` / `[research]` / `[wisher]`)

**[answered]** — resolved now via logic or extant code:

- **who runs the sweep** — `[answered]`: the hook detects-and-nudges (exit 2 when stale); the
  *clone* runs the `learn.domain.terms` skill. a 5s shell hook cannot do reflective
  term-discovery. precedent: `memory.guard`. → hook nudges, clone sweeps.
- **how `domain.terms/` reaches context** — `[answered]`: `boot.yml` enumerates files under
  `always.briefs.say`; it does not scan arbitrary dirs. so `domain.terms/` must be **declared in
  `boot.yml`** (or the term files nest under `briefs/`). a code fact, not a wisher call.
- **what "born" means / scope** — `[answered]`: the dictionary is scoped to the **words
  (nouns/verbs/adjs) that compose the domain objects & operations declared in this repo** — the
  noun `stone` (from `Stone`, `getStone`), the verb `gen` (from every `gen*`), etc. the
  declared dobjs/dops are the **anchor**; the **term is the word** decomposed from them, one
  entry per word, reused across all its declarations. exclude generic English and vocab
  **imported** from a `package.json` dependency (`DomainEntity`, `IsoPriceWords`, `Role` are the
  tool's terms, not this domain's).
- **what grounds "terms engaged with"** — `[answered]`: ground the sweep in the **git diff of
  touched files**, not brain recall alone (compaction can erode recall; the repo runs a
  `postcompact` hook as proof). → git diff is the spine.
- **term-file granularity** — `[answered]`: a **`choice._.` cluster** per term — a say-level
  `term=<x>._.choice._.md` (always booted, lean) + a required ref-level `term=<x>._.choice.reason.md`,
  extensible with one-per-example `term=<x>._.choice.example=<abc>.md` (see decisions + section C).
  the say/ref split keeps the always-booted footprint lean while the full record (etymology,
  disputes, evidence, examples) lives on demand.

**[research]** — verify in a later phase:

- **findsert atomicity under concurrent sweeps** — `[research]`: two clones that stop at once
  could race on the same term file; verify how the repo's extant findserts handle concurrent
  create and adopt their approach.
- **how the learner harvests terms from declared dobjs/dops** — `[research]`: the anchor signals
  are cheap (`extends DomainEntity`/`DomainLiteral`, a `domain.operation` file); the open part is
  the **split of a dobj/dop name into its constituent terms** (`getStone` → verb `get` + noun
  `stone`) so each word is itemized once. verify the tidiest way for the learner to do this split.

**[decided]** — settled by the wisher (2026-07-22):

- **staleness threshold** — `[decided]`: **1 hour, walltime, fixed** for now (not configurable).
- **synonym enforcement severity** — `[decided → dissolved]`: the question presumed the sweep
  *enforces*. it does not. **the sweep does no work on its own** — it only nudges; the **driver
  decides** what to do. the sole requirement is that the learner **articulates their thoughts
  into `progress.md`**. so there is no "auto-forbid vs propose" for a mechanism to make.
- **scope of "engaged with"** — `[decided → dissolved]`: likewise **up to the learner** to
  decide (wrote? read? debated?). the sweep is a nudge, not an arbiter. git diff is a *hint* the
  learner may lean on, not a rule the sweep imposes.
- **dispute pattern** — `[decided]`: **establish the pattern by example.** the briefs must
  **demonstrate a term dispute** and set the convention; a dispute lives in the term's
  **`choice.reason`** file (ref-level). a separate **`howto` brief** carries the dispute template.
  a proposed example is in section C below.
- **term-file evidence depth** — `[decided]`: a **`choice._.` cluster, two levels.** the
  **`choice._.md` is say-level** (concise, always booted into context); the **required
  `choice.reason.md` is ref-level** (deep: etymology, disputes, evidence — surfaced on demand),
  extensible with one-per-example `choice.example=<abc>.md`. establish this via the cluster
  templates (proposed in section C).

### adjacencies surfaced by self-review (for the next stone)

- **extant per-term-forbid convention (distinct — do NOT merge)** — the mechanic role ships
  `rule.forbid.term=<word>.md` briefs enforced at write-time by the `forbid-terms.blocklist`
  hook. that is the mechanic's choice for banning *generic* words. this wish's domain-term
  forbid is deliberately **not** a write-time gate — it is a mandate honored by the learner's
  distillation + cleanup-when-disturbed + review. keep them separate; do not turn the
  domain-term forbid into a write blocker.
- **the ambiguous-overload forbid** — `rule.forbid.term.addition.ambiguous` is the twin of the
  synonym-forbid; the wish scopes to synonyms only, but the design space includes overload.
- **deterministic vs probabilistic split** — the dir scaffold (the rule *symlinks* + `.readme`)
  + findsert are deterministic; the term-*discovery* that feeds them is a probabilistic
  `imagine*`-grade judgment. do not imply the whole sweep is deterministic.

## what is awkward?

- **"stale > 1 hour → hold the stop"** is a time-based heuristic on an event-based hook.
  as a gentle reminder — take a moment to distill after a good round — it fits the owl's "tea
  first" ethos far better than a punitive block. still, a hard time threshold on a creative
  session is a blunt trigger, and the pause interrupts. the grace window softens it. tradeoff:
  a nudge to tend the dictionary vs. flow.
- **the learner's core judgment is the fuzzy part** — "which terms (nouns/verbs/adjs) do this
  session's dobjs/dops introduce?" and "is this a synonym or a distinct concept?" are exactly the
  domain-discovery judgments the architect briefs treat as *hard*. the dobj/dop anchor helps
  (a `DomainEntity`/`DomainLiteral` extend or a `domain.operation` file is a concrete signal),
  but the split-into-words and the synonym call stay fuzzy. mitigation: disputes + "left in place
  until disturbed"
  keep mistakes reversible.
- **`progress.md` is the one required artifact** — since it is the sole hard requirement and the
  hook's only trigger, the learner must always end a distillation by articulating it (even to say
  "no new terms, because…"). if the learner writes term files but forgets `progress.md`, the hook
  keeps reminding — so the guidance must make the `progress.md` articulation the *last,
  guaranteed* step.
- **the synonym-forbid** could feel strong for a role whose vibe is the patient owl — but it is
  softened three ways: it binds *contracts* only (comments stay free), it is a mandate not a
  write-gate, and "open into dispute" is a first-class escape valve. keep that dispute path
  low-friction, so the forbid guides rather than scolds.
- **cache path names a specific role twice** (`repo=bhrain/role=learner`) while the terms
  live under `role=any` — a slight asymmetry: the *sweeper* is the learner, the *glossary*
  is everyone's. worth naming explicitly so it doesn't read as inconsistent.
