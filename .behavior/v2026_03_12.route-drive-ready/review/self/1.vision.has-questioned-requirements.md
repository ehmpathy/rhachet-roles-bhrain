# self-review: has-questioned-requirements

## the vision's requirements

the vision proposes two changes:
1. clearer normal guidance: "done? run this to proceed"
2. nudge after 5 hooks: explicit "walk the way" message

## requirements questioned

### 1. "done? run this to proceed" — is this the right frame?

**the requirement:** change guidance from "are we there yet? if so, run" to "done? run this to proceed"

**what i question:**
- does "done?" still invite hesitation? a clone unsure of its work might still interpret this as "am i done? i don't know, you tell me"
- the word "proceed" implies forward motion after completion — but the command's purpose is to *test* completion, not confirm it

**alternative considered:**
- "ready? run this to check" — emphasizes the command validates, not assumes
- "run this to check your work" — removes the question entirely

**verdict:** "done? run this to proceed" is clearer than before, but still asks a question. the nudge compensates with explicit instruction. acceptable for now, can iterate.

### 2. "5 hooks" threshold — why 5?

**the requirement:** after 5 hooks without passage attempt, show nudge

**what i question:**
- why 5 and not 3? a clone stuck for 3 hooks is already in trouble
- why 5 and not 10? maybe 5 is too aggressive for slow, methodical clones
- is there data on how many hooks a normal passage takes?

**research:**
- the example case showed 21+ hook stops before escalation to human
- 5 is ~25% of the way to escalation — seems reasonable as early intervention

**verdict:** 5 is arbitrary but defensible. it's early enough to help, late enough to not annoy. can tune based on observed behavior.

### 3. "walk the way" phrase — will clones understand?

**the requirement:** nudge uses "walk the way" metaphor

**what i question:**
- is this too poetic? will clones parse "walk the way" as instruction or decoration?
- the nudge also says "YOU run this command, don't wait" — that's the clear part
- "walk the way" might be noise that clones skip over

**verdict:** "walk the way" is flavor. the explicit "YOU run this" carries the instruction. if clones miss the metaphor, they still get the directive. low risk.

### 4. state tracker — is per-stone sufficient?

**the requirement:** track `hooksWithoutAttempt` per stone in `.route/drive.state.$stone.json`

**what i question:**
- what happens on context compaction? does the clone "forget" it's been stuck?
- is per-stone the right granularity? or should it be per-session?
- what if a clone legitimately needs 10+ hooks to finish a complex stone?

**analysis:**
- compaction: the hook fires regardless of clone memory. state persists in filesystem.
- per-stone is correct — different stones have different complexity
- for complex stones: nudge is gentle. "walk the way" doesn't force, just reminds.

**verdict:** per-stone state is appropriate. filesystem persistence survives compaction.

### 5. no escalation tiers — should nudge intensify?

**the requirement:** same nudge at hooks 5, 6, 7... up to 21

**what i question:**
- should nudge escalate? e.g., more urgent at 10, alert at 15?
- or is flat nudge better — avoid clones that learn to wait for escalation?

**verdict:** flat nudge is simpler. if clone ignores 16 nudges, it will hit the 21-hook escalation anyway. no need for intermediate tiers.

## requirements i did NOT find in vision

### what about approval-gated stones?

the original wish mentioned clones that freeze specifically on approval stones. the vision addresses *all* stones, not just approval-gated ones.

**is this correct?** yes — the root cause was ambiguous guidance, not approval gates specifically. the fix applies universally.

### what about the `--as approvable` idea?

dropped. the vision explicitly says "no new commands, no new terminology." clearer guidance solves the problem without new flags.

## conclusion

all requirements questioned. no blockers found. the vision is coherent and addresses the root cause.
