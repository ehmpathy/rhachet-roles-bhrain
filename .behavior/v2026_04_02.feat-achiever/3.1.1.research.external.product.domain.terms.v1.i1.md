# research: domain terms — achiever role

## objective

coin terms for new domain objects that:
- maximize specificity → eliminate ambiguity & minimize confusion
- maximize intuition → eliminate friction & maximize adoption

to create a ubiquitous language.

---

## entity: the desired end state

### candidates

| term | origin | domain | pros | cons |
|------|--------|--------|------|------|
| Goal | 1530s, "end point of a race" [1] | universal | intuitive, emotionally resonant | abstract, not inherently measurable |
| Objective | military/PM | corporate, military | implies measurability | clinical, overused |
| Outcome | healthcare, PM | healthcare, education | results-focused | passive (happens to you) |
| Target | archery | sales, metrics | concrete, visual | implies a number |
| Mission | military | military, corporate | inspires action | grandiose for small tasks |
| Quest | games | games, RPGs | adventure metaphor | juvenile connotations |

### etymology deep dive: Goal

**quote [1]:** "the word 'goal' appears from the 1530s as 'end point of a race,' of uncertain origin. possibly from Old English *gal ('obstacle, barrier')." — etymonline

**insight:** "goal" originally meant the boundary or barrier at the end of a race — the line you cross. the sports connotation (soccer goal) came later. the original sense aligns with achiever: a goal is the boundary between incomplete and complete.

### convergence signal

**sources [1, 3, 4] agree:** "goal" is the most universal and intuitive term. academic research uses "goal" (locke & latham). OKRs use "objective" but define it as a special type of goal.

### recommendation: **Goal**

- universally understood
- emotionally resonant
- maps to "desired state" in cognitive science
- short, punchy, lowercase-friendly

## entity: peer input to be processed

### candidates

| term | origin | domain | pros | cons |
|------|--------|--------|------|------|
| Ask | Old English *āscian* [22] | business | concise, neutral, action-oriented | jargon-y in some contexts |
| Request | formal English | customer service | formal, clear | soft, may be ignored |
| Requirement | tech | software | implies necessity | rigid, no flexibility |
| Need | economics | sales | urgency | often misused |
| Want | economics | sales | honest | low priority connotation |
| Wish | fantasy | MoSCoW [18] | low-pressure | implies optionality |
| Demand | economics | sales | urgent | aggressive tone |
| Order | military, healthcare | military, medical | authoritative | requires authority |
| Ticket | IT support | support, field service | trackable | impersonal, transactional |

### etymology deep dive: Ask

**quote [22]:** "a similar phonological change gave us the word 'ask', which was originally 'ax' (or 'acs' or 'ahs'). Beowulf and Chaucer both use 'ax'. we didn't settle on 'ask' as the standard form until the time of Shakespeare." — arrant pedantry

**quote [17]:** "'ask' is shorter, punchier, and fits easily into fast-paced speech and business jargon... 'request' carries formal/soft connotations; 'demand' is strongly adversarial, while 'ask' sits in the middle." — language log

### conflict: Ask vs Request

**sources [17] vs general usage:** "ask" as a noun ("the ask") is business jargon that some find annoying. "request" is more formal but softer. for achiever, we need the brain to treat peer input seriously — "ask" implies a response is deserved.

### recommendation: **Ask**

- short, punchy, fits $noun.$adj pattern
- neutral tone (neither aggressive nor dismissive)
- commonly used as noun in business ("what's the ask?")
- aligns with wish document language

## entity: link between ask and goal

### candidates

| term | origin | domain | pros | cons |
|------|--------|--------|------|------|
| Coverage | insurance, QA | QA, insurance | implies completeness | abstract |
| Link | general | web, databases | simple | generic |
| Resolution | legal, support | customer service, legal | implies finality | conflated with "solved" |
| Trace | tech | audit, debug | audit connotation | technical |
| Assignment | work | project management | clear ownership | implies person, not goal |

### recommendation: **Coverage**

- implies "covered by" relationship
- QA connotation: all cases must be covered
- aligns with `asks.coverage.jsonl` filename
- question "is this ask covered?" is intuitive

## literal: verification criterion

### candidates

| term | origin | domain | pros | cons |
|------|--------|--------|------|------|
| Gate | software | DevOps, QA | clear pass/fail | binary, no partial |
| Criterion | academic | science | precise | plural is awkward |
| Test | tech | QA | actionable | implies automation |
| Check | general | audit | simple | too simple |
| Acceptance | agile | Scrum | user-focused | long word |
| Definition of Done | Scrum | agile | complete | phrase, not word |

### etymology deep dive: Gate

**quote [7]:** "quality gates act as checkpoints throughout software development. each stage must meet specific criteria before code advances to the next phase." — testRigor

**insight:** a gate is a physical barrier that opens when conditions are met. the metaphor is powerful: you cannot pass until the gate opens. this aligns with achiever's `how.gate` — the brain must pass through verification.

### recommendation: **Gate**

- physical metaphor (barrier that opens)
- short, fits $noun.$adj pattern
- pass/fail semantic is appropriate
- already used in vision document

## literal: status choices

### candidates from research

| status | definition | source |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Blocked | "an obstacle stands in the way" [24] | flow, jira |
| Queued | "in queue, awaited" [24] | general |
| In Progress | "work in progress" [24] | general |
| Active | synonym for in progress | general |
| Done | "work required has been finished" [24] | general |
| Complete | synonym for done | general |
| Fulfilled | "request has been completed or satisfied" [10] | e-commerce |
| Resolved | "settled or locked-in" (promises) [10] | code |

### vision document choices

| status | sense |
|--------|---------|
| blocked | cannot proceed, has blocker |
| enqueued | awaited, in queue |
| inflight | work in progress |
| fulfilled | complete with evidence |

### analysis

**blocked** — universal, clear impediment signal. matches research [24].

**enqueued** — more precise than "queued" (which is ambiguous). implies a queue with order. technical but accurate.

**inflight** — aviation metaphor. clearer than "in progress" (which is two words). implies motion toward destination.

**fulfilled** — more formal than "done". implies satisfaction of the ask. aligns with e-commerce/logistics usage [10].

### recommendation: keep **blocked, enqueued, inflight, fulfilled**

- four states cover the lifecycle
- each is a single word (fits flag filename)
- each has distinct semantic
- avoids generic "done" (fulfilled implies evidence)

## parallel concepts from other domains

### blue collar: work order

**quote [11]:** "a work order is a task or job for a customer that can be scheduled and assigned." — salesforce

**insight:** work orders have a requester, an assignee, a description, and a completion status. goals are similar but originate from the brain itself (not an external system).

### construction: punch list

**quote [12]:** "it's called a 'punch list' because historically contractors would manually punch holes next to each item on a paper list to show that a task was complete." — procore

**insight:** the physical act to punch a hole marks completion. for achiever, the flag file is the punch — a visible marker of status.

### healthcare: treatment plan / goals of care

**quote [13]:** "goals of care is a term commonly used to refer to the entire process: values, goals, and treatment preferences." — NIH

**insight:** healthcare distinguishes values (why), goals (what), and preferences (how). achiever mirrors this with `why`, `what`, `how` in the Goal schema.

### military: mission / commander's intent

**quote [14]:** "a mission is a clear, concise, simple statement on the task the unit must accomplish and the purpose behind it. answers the '5 Ws'." — US Marines

**quote [15]:** "commander's intent is a clear expression of the purpose of the operation and the desired end state." — army war college

**insight:** military separates the intent (why/what) from the specific orders (how). achiever's `why.purpose` and `what.outcome` align with commander's intent; `how.task` aligns with specific orders.

### game design: quest

**quote [23]:** "the primary mechanical goal of a quest is to give players an objective with a reward that encourages them to progress through the game world." — game developer

**insight:** quests have objectives, rewards, and completion criteria. achiever's goals have outcomes (objective), benefits (reward), and gates (completion criteria).

## anti-patterns from research

### anti-pattern 1: overloaded terms

**sources [6, 17] warn:** "need" is often misused (conflated with want). "demand" sounds aggressive. "requirement" implies no flexibility.

**achiever decision:** use "ask" — neutral, clear, not overloaded.

### anti-pattern 2: ambiguous status

**sources [24] warn:** "queued" is ambiguous (awaited for what?). "in progress" is two words.

**achiever decision:** use precise single-word statuses: blocked, enqueued, inflight, fulfilled.

### anti-pattern 3: abstract verification

**sources [7, 9] warn:** "acceptance criteria" can become checkbox exercises. "definition of done" is generic.

**achiever decision:** use "gate" — implies physical barrier, pass/fail, must be articulated per goal.

## final term recommendations

| concept | recommended term | rationale |
|---------|------------------|-----------|
| desired end state | **Goal** | universal, emotionally resonant, etymologically "end point of race" |
| peer input | **Ask** | concise, neutral, commonly used as noun |
| ask-to-goal link | **Coverage** | implies completeness, QA connotation |
| verification criterion | **Gate** | physical barrier metaphor, pass/fail |
| status: cannot proceed | **blocked** | universal, clear impediment |
| status: in queue | **enqueued** | precise, implies ordered wait |
| status: in progress | **inflight** | aviation metaphor, single word |
| status: complete | **fulfilled** | formal, implies evidence |

## treestruct shape

```
Goal
├── slug (identifier)
├── why
│   ├── ask (verbatim input)
│   ├── purpose (motivation)
│   └── benefit (what success enables)
├── what
│   └── outcome (desired end state)
├── how
│   ├── task (next action)
│   └── gate (verification criterion)
├── status
│   ├── choice (blocked|enqueued|inflight|fulfilled)
│   └── reason (evidence or blocker)
├── when (optional)
│   ├── goal (blocked by another goal)
│   └── event (blocked by external event)
├── source (peer:human|peer:robot|self)
├── createdAt
└── updatedAt
```
