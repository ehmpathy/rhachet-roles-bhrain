wish =

we should

1. require drivers to --articulate their responses to each peer review, before it can be continued

2. enable reviewers to be given a --conversation $conversation as part of the guard definition, which the reviewers can then use to resolve the filepaths of the full conversation


---

the response files should be articulated as 

feedback.{given,taken}

where today the reviewers write only to the .given output dir

the drivers will be required to articulate into the .taken output dir

for each iteration, before it can be progressed (same key as the given key, with the only difference being .given vs .taken)

---

that way, its easy to find the full conversation as well.

---

the conversation should be supported natively by the `review` skill

but also, the $conversation flag should be supported natively by guard reviewrs, so that any skill can leverage the full glob of filepaths of the conversation

---

why do we want this?
- to eliminate the risk that a driver wastes budget by ignoring feedback given
- to decrease the infinite churn that reviewrs sometimes have, when they cant see the direct responses that the drivers gave them

---

notably, --conversation $conversation is opt-in, so if a specific reviewer is decided to work better blind without visibility of past rounds, thats defo supported

---

exhaustive boundary test coverage is the most important part
