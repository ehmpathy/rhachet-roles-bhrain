# research: claims — achiever role

## claim.1 [FACT] — goals with specific outcomes outperform vague intentions

**claim:** goals that specify a concrete outcome produce better results than goals that state only a direction.

**evidence:**
- locke & latham's 35-year research program on goal theory shows specific, difficult goals lead to higher performance than "do your best" goals (locke & latham, 2002)
- meta-analysis across 40,000+ participants confirms effect size d = 0.42-0.80 for specific vs vague goals (locke & latham, 2006)

**sources:**
- locke, e. a., & latham, g. p. (2002). "build a practically useful theory of goal set and task motivation." american psychologist, 57(9), 705-717.
- locke, e. a., & latham, g. p. (2006). "new directions in goal theory." current directions in psychological science, 15(5), 265-268.

**relevance:** the achiever role requires goals with `what.outcome` — a concrete end state, not a direction.

---

## claim.2 [FACT] — goals require verification criteria to be complete

**claim:** a goal without a verification criterion is incomplete and prone to scope drift.

**evidence:**
- doran's SMART framework (1981) established "measurable" and "achievable" as core goal properties
- the gate (verification criterion) prevents "scope creep" by bound constraints on what "done" means

**sources:**
- doran, g. t. (1981). "there's a S.M.A.R.T. way to write management's goals and objectives." management review, 70(11), 35-36.

**relevance:** the achiever role requires `how.gate` — explicit verification criteria for each goal.

---

## claim.3 [SUMP] — SMART goals may constrain creative work

**claim:** the SMART framework (specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound) can inhibit breakthrough work that requires exploration.

**evidence:**
- amabile's research on creativity shows that overly rigid constraints reduce intrinsic motivation (amabile, 1996)
- "stretch goals" research suggests some ambiguity enables innovation (sitkin et al., 2011)

**sources:**
- amabile, t. m. (1996). "creativity in context." westview press.
- sitkin, s. b., et al. (2011). "the paradox of stretch goals: organizations in pursuit of the seemingly impossible." academy of management review, 36(3), 544-566.

**relevance:** the achiever role should support both specific gates (for clear tasks) and exploratory gates (for creative work). the `how.gate` field can describe verification as "explore 3 approaches and select best" rather than a rigid metric.

---

## claim.4 [FACT] — approach goals outperform avoidance goals

**claim:** goals framed as "move toward X" produce better outcomes than goals framed as "avoid Y."

**evidence:**
- elliot & mcgregor's 2x2 achievement goal framework shows approach motivation correlates with higher performance and well-being (elliot & mcgregor, 2001)
- avoidance goals increase anxiety and reduce persistence

**sources:**
- elliot, a. j., & mcgregor, h. a. (2001). "a 2x2 achievement goal framework." journal of personality and social psychology, 80(3), 501-519.

**relevance:** the achiever role should bias toward approach goals. `what.outcome` should describe a positive end state, not what to avoid.

---

## claim.5 [FACT] — LLMs can decompose goals but may hallucinate subtasks

**claim:** large language models can break down high-level goals into subtasks, but may invent plausible-seemd subtasks that are unnecessary or incorrect.

**evidence:**
- autogpt and babyagi experiments show LLMs can generate task lists from goals, but often produce verbose or irrelevant subtasks (nakajima, 2023; richards, 2023)
- "hallucinate" in this context means the model asserts subtasks that sound reasonable but don't match the actual goal scope

**sources:**
- nakajima, y. (2023). "babyagi." github repository.
- richards, t. (2023). "auto-gpt: an autonomous gpt-4 experiment." github repository.

**relevance:** the achiever role requires the brain itself to triage goals (with full context), not a nested LLM call that lacks conversation history.

---

## claim.6 [KHUE] — goals and tasks are distinct concepts

**claim:** a goal describes a desired end state; a task describes an action to take. conflation leads to confusion.

**evidence:**
- cognitive science distinguishes "goal state" (what) from "operator" (how) in problem representation (newell & simon, 1972)
- project management separates "objectives" (outcomes) from "work breakdown" (tasks)

**sources:**
- newell, a., & simon, h. a. (1972). "human problem solve." prentice-hall.

**relevance:** the achiever role separates `what.outcome` (the goal state) from `how.task` (the next action). this prevents the common failure mode of "i did the task but didn't achieve the goal."

---

## claim.7 [KHUE] — wishes and goals are distinct concepts

**claim:** a wish is a desire without commitment; a goal is a commitment with a path. transformation from wish to goal requires articulation.

**evidence:**
- oettingen's research on mental contrast shows that wishes without implementation plans rarely convert to action (oettingen, 2012)
- the wish → goal transformation requires "if-then" commitment (gollwitzer, 1999)

**sources:**
- oettingen, g. (2012). "future thought and behaviour change." european review of social psychology, 23(1), 1-63.
- gollwitzer, p. m. (1999). "implementation intentions: strong effects of simple plans." american psychologist, 54(7), 493-503.

**relevance:** the achiever role forces articulation via the goal schema. a vague wish ("make it better") must become a goal with why, what, and how. the schema transformation is the commitment mechanism.

---

## claim.8 [OPIN] — urgency and importance are orthogonal dimensions

**claim:** goals should be evaluated on both urgency (time pressure) and importance (value), and these dimensions are independent.

**evidence:**
- the eisenhower matrix (urgent × important) is widely used in productivity systems
- covey popularized the framework in "the 7 habits of highly effective people" (1989)

**sources:**
- covey, s. r. (1989). "the 7 habits of highly effective people." free press.

**relevance:** the achiever role does not yet include priority. this claim suggests future iteration should add urgency and importance dimensions for goal sort.

---

## claim.9 [FACT] — goal dependencies create critical paths

**claim:** when goal B depends on goal A, A must complete before B can start. this creates a critical path that constrains schedule.

**evidence:**
- project management theory formalizes this as precedence constraint (kerzner, 2017)
- software development uses "blocked by" and "blocks" relationships in issue trackers

**sources:**
- kerzner, h. (2017). "project management: a systems approach to plan, schedule, and control." wiley.

**relevance:** the achiever role supports `when.goal` for blocked goals. this enables dependency chains.

---

## claim.10 [SUMP] — explicit goal articulation may slow fast-movers

**claim:** require explicit goal articulation adds friction that may slow down brains that already understand the task.

**evidence:**
- expert performance research shows that experts often operate on tacit knowledge that resists articulation (dreyfus & dreyfus, 1986)
- "analysis paralysis" occurs when articulation requirements exceed the complexity of the task

**sources:**
- dreyfus, h. l., & dreyfus, s. e. (1986). "mind over machine." free press.

**relevance:** the achiever role should balance thoroughness with speed. for simple, clear tasks, the brain may provide terse articulation. the schema enforces structure, not verbosity.

---

## claim.11 [FACT] — context loss degrades goal pursuit

**claim:** when context is lost (e.g., memory clear, session restart), goal pursuit degrades because the brain loses track of what was asked and why.

**evidence:**
- human memory research shows "prospective memory" (memory for intentions) is fragile and benefits from external cues (einstein & mcdaniel, 2005)
- ai agent experiments show that context window limits cause goal forget in long sessions

**sources:**
- einstein, g. o., & mcdaniel, m. a. (2005). "prospective memory: multiple retrieval processes." current directions in psychological science, 14(6), 286-290.

**relevance:** the achiever role persists goals to filesystem precisely to survive context loss. `.goals/` is the external cue that enables goal recall across sessions.

---

## claim.12 [KHUE] — append-only logs preserve causal history

**claim:** append-only logs (like JSONL) preserve the causal sequence of events, which aids diagnosis when goals are abandoned or recomposed.

**evidence:**
- event source architecture uses append-only logs to enable replay and audit (fowler, 2005)
- git's append-only object store enables history trace

**sources:**
- fowler, m. (2005). "event source." martinfowler.com.

**relevance:** the achiever role uses `asks.inventory.jsonl` (append-only) to preserve the sequence of peer input. this enables diagnosis of abandoned asks and context reconstruction.
