# research: premortem — achiever role

## premortem: imagine we shipped and it failed

we shipped achiever, and it failed badly. why?

### scenario 1: triage friction kills adoption

the brain resents the triage ceremony. every session end becomes a ritual:
- "uncovered asks detected"
- brain must create goals for each
- brain starts to shortcut — creates shallow goals just to pass
- goals become meaningless checklist items
- humans lose trust because goals don't reflect real intent

**why it happened:** we optimized for "no ask lost" at the cost of natural flow.

### scenario 2: goal proliferation creates noise

every ask becomes a goal. the `.goals/` directory explodes:
- 50 goals after a week
- brain can't find relevant goals
- humans open `.goals/` and see chaos
- fulfilled goals mix with active goals
- no one knows what's actually important

**why it happened:** we didn't design for goal lifecycle (archive, prune, prioritize).

### scenario 3: vague asks produce useless goals

human says "make it better." brain creates goal:
- why.ask: "make it better"
- why.purpose: "human wants improvement"
- what.outcome: "it is better"
- how.gate: "human confirms it's better"

the goal exists but provides no direction. brain works on random "improvements." human is disappointed.

**why it happened:** schema forces fields but can't force clarity.

---

## what obvious signs did we ignore?

- **sign:** the vision document mentions "terse articulation for simple tasks" but we require full schema anyway
- **sign:** the wish mentions "self trust is fine for now" but we force explicit coverage for every ask
- **sign:** llm quality varies — we assumed reliable inference without fallback
- **sign:** humans often change their mind mid-session — we assumed asks are immutable

---

## what assumptions could turn out wrong?

| assumption | what if wrong? | likelihood |
|------------|----------------|------------|
| brain can reliably detect goals from natural language | vague asks produce vague goals | medium-high |
| triage at session end is acceptable friction | brain resents ceremony, shortcuts emerge | medium |
| file-based persistence is sufficient | too many files, no query capability | low-medium |
| goals are atomic (one ask = one goal) | complex asks need decomposition | medium |
| status.choice (4 values) covers all states | edge cases emerge (paused, delegated, etc.) | low |
| humans want transparency via `.goals/` | humans never look, clutter becomes a nuisance | medium |

---

## what is the nightmare scenario?

**the nightmare:** achiever makes brains worse, not better.

the brain becomes so focused on triage ceremony that it:
- loses flow mid-task to create goals
- creates shallow goals that don't help
- annoys humans with "triage required" blocks
- slows every session without clear benefit

humans disable achiever. the role is deprecated. effort wasted.

**why it's bad:**
- credibility lost for future role development
- humans learn to distrust goal-track mechanisms
- hard to try again — "we tried that, it failed"

---

## what mitigations to consider?

| risk | mitigation | cost of mitigation |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| triage friction | allow "quick triage" mode that covers asks without full schema | medium — needs schema relaxation |
| goal proliferation | auto-archive fulfilled goals to `.goals/.archive/` | low — simple file move |
| vague asks | require gates to be verifiable (not "human confirms") | low — validation rule |
| noise from old goals | add `--active` filter to goal.memory.get | low — query parameter |
| brain shortcuts schema | detect shallow fields and warn | medium — heuristic detection |
| human never checks `.goals/` | surface goal count in triage output | low — display change |
| llm inference varies | allow manual goal creation without inference | already supported |

---

## priority mitigations for v1

1. **auto-archive fulfilled goals** — prevents clutter without effort
2. **`--active` filter on goal.memory.get** — brain sees only relevant goals
3. **warn on shallow gates** — "human confirms" is not verifiable

these three address the highest-likelihood, highest-impact risks.

---

## risks we accept for v1

| risk | why we accept it |
|------|------------------|
| triage friction | we need data on whether it's really friction; can relax later |
| vague asks produce vague goals | this is an llm quality issue, not an achiever design issue |
| goal proliferation | auto-archive mitigates; full lifecycle is v2 scope |

---

## conclusion

the most plausible failure mode is **triage friction kills adoption**.

mitigation strategy:
1. monitor brain behavior — do they shortcut schemas?
2. be ready to relax schema requirements if friction is too high
3. auto-archive fulfilled goals to reduce clutter
4. surface active goal count to give humans confidence without them having to inspect files

achiever succeeds if brains adopt it naturally. if it feels like a chore, it fails.
