# reflection: premortem

## premortem: imagine we shipped and it failed

project forward: we shipped this, and it failed badly. why?

1. **clones still hesitate** — the `--as arrived` frame didn't change behavior. clones still interpret it as judgment, or something else causes hesitation.

2. **nudge triggers at wrong time** — 7 hooks is either too early (annoys engaged clones) or too late (stuck clones already exhausted context).

3. **quote is too abstract** — "do your work, then step back" doesn't register with clones. they parse it as decoration, not instruction.

---

## what obvious signs did we ignore?

- **one example case**: we only have one documented freeze case. may not represent the full problem space.
- **no A/B test**: we can't compare `--as arrived` vs `--as passed` effectiveness.
- **human projection**: we assume "arrived" feels more natural, but we're not clones.

---

## what assumptions could turn out wrong?

| assumption | what if wrong? | likelihood |
|------------|----------------|------------|
| clones hesitate due to "passed" frame | clones hesitate for other reasons (training, instructions) | medium |
| 7 is the right threshold | 7 is too early or too late | low |
| drum nudge helps | clones ignore the nudge | low |
| zen quote resonates | clones parse it as noise | medium |
| `--as arrived` feels natural | clones still hesitate on "arrived" | low |

---

## what is the nightmare scenario?

**worst outcome:** we ship `--as arrived`, clones still freeze, AND we've added complexity (new state file, new alias) without benefit. the codebase is more complex, the problem persists, and we've spent effort on a null result.

**damage:**
- wasted development time
- added complexity without value
- false confidence that problem is solved
- wishers still face frozen clones

**recovery:**
- revert the change (alias, state file)
- investigate other root causes
- try different interventions

---

## what mitigations to consider?

| risk | mitigation | cost of mitigation |
|------|------------|--------------------|
| clone still hesitates | monitor freeze rate after ship | low (observability) |
| wrong threshold | make threshold configurable | medium (more code) |
| quote ignored | test with real clones before full rollout | low (manual test) |
| alias adds confusion | document clearly, keep backwards compat | low (docs) |
| state corruption | add state file validation, cleanup on pass | medium (more code) |

---

## decision

**proceed with caution.**

the risk is manageable:
- backwards compat preserved (`--as passed` still works)
- state file is per-stone, isolated
- threshold can be tuned later
- worst case: revert

the potential upside is high:
- clones stop freeze behavior
- wishers have smoother experience
- route completion accelerates
