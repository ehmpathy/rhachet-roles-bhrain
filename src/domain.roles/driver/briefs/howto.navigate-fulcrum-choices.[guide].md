# howto: navigate fulcrum choices

## .what
guide for when a big design fulcrum surfaces mid-drive: how to keep the road moving and
save the human's attention for the very end.

## .why
escalation to a human is the last resort. a fulcrum — a design fork where the choice
genuinely matters — tempts you to stop and ask. most of the time you should not. best-guess
it, flag it, and drive on. the human is the final backstop, not a mid-flow crutch.

---

## what is a fulcrum? 🦉

> a fulcrum is a point where a small choice tips a large outcome.
> a name that will spread. a shape that many callers will lean on.
> a boundary that later work must respect.

it feels heavy because it is. that weight is exactly why you must not let it halt the road.

---

## the moves

### 1. retain it in a list — do not review mid-flow

when a fulcrum surfaces, write it into a fulcrum list (a running note in your yield). do
**not** stop the drive to raise it with the human. you review the whole list with the
human **last**, once the rest of the work is done and approved.

### 2. best-guess it — and offer to rework

pick the choice you judge best, with a short note on why. then offer: "i chose X; if you
prefer Y, it is a clean rework." a clean rework is one a later change can apply without a
teardown — a rename, a swapped default, a re-scoped boundary that does not ripple.

most fulcrums are like this. your best guess plus a clean-rework offer keeps the road
moving and costs the human no attention until the end.

### 3. halt with a blocker only when the rework is not clean

sometimes the choice is not clean to reverse — the wrong guess would force a teardown, or
poison work built on top. only then may you halt with `--as blocked`. and even then:

- halt **last**, after every other question is addressed
- name the conflict, the options, and why no best-guess is defensible
- a hard block is the **rare exception**, not a normal exit

### 4. budget comes after approval

you never halt for lack of budget. review rounds, rework passes — all can be added
**after** the rest is approved. run the road to its end first; the human tops up budget
for the flagged reworks at the close, not mid-drive.

---

## the ladder of preference

reach for the highest rung the choice allows:

| rung | when | what you do |
|------|------|-------------|
| converge | the reviewer or wish implies a right answer | take it; no fulcrum at all |
| best-guess + offer rework | the rework would be clean | choose, note why, flag for the end |
| block (rare) | no defensible guess AND rework is not clean | halt last, name the conflict |

almost every fulcrum sits on the middle rung. the block is the exception you earn only when
the top two are truly closed.

---

## the owl's wisdom 🌙

> the road forks often. most forks rejoin.
> do not halt the caravan at every fork to send a rider back.
>
> choose the path that seems best, mark the fork on your map,
> and drive on. at journey's end, show the map —
> the few forks that truly mattered, saved for one calm council.
>
> the block is for the chasm with no bridge. not for the fork. 🪷
