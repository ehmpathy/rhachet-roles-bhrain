# research: external product references

## reference.1: tversky & kahneman - prospect theory

> "Prospect theory, proposed by Tversky and Kahneman, recognizes that people make biased or inconsistent decisions in real life based on how options are presented—or, in other words, framed."

**citation:** Tversky, A., & Kahneman, D. (1981). The Framing of Decisions and the Psychology of Choice. Science, 211(4481), 453-458.

**application:** the `--as arrived` vs `--as passed` change is a direct application of prospect theory. same outcome, different frame.

## reference.2: thaler & sunstein - nudge

> "Nudge theory is a concept in behavioral economics that proposes adaptive designs of the decision environment (choice architecture) as ways to influence the behavior and decision-making."

**citation:** Thaler, R. H., & Sunstein, C. R. (2008). Nudge: Improving Decisions About Health, Wealth, and Happiness. Yale University Press.

**application:** the drum nudge after 7 hooks is a choice architecture intervention. it gently reminds without forcing.

## reference.3: tao te ching - wu wei

> "do your work, then step back. the only path to serenity."

**citation:** Lao Tzu. Tao Te Ching, Chapter 9.

**application:** the zen quote in the nudge embodies wu wei — effortless action. it reminds clones to act naturally, not overthink.
